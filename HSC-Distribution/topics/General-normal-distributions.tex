% Topic: General Normal Distributions

If $X \sim N(\mu, \sigma^2)$, the pdf of $X$ is
\[
f(x) = \frac{1}{\sigma\sqrt{2\pi}}\,\exp\!\left(-\frac{(x-\mu)^2}{2\sigma^2}\right).
\]

\textbf{Standardising.} To compute probabilities, convert $X$ to the standard normal variable $Z$:
\[
Z = \frac{X - \mu}{\sigma} \sim N(0,1).
\]
Then
\[
P(a \leq X \leq b) = P\!\left(\frac{a-\mu}{\sigma} \leq Z \leq \frac{b-\mu}{\sigma}\right).
\]

\textbf{The 68-95-99.7 rule.} For any normal distribution:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item approximately $68\%$ of values lie within one standard deviation of the mean.
  \item approximately $95\%$ of values lie within two standard deviations of the mean.
  \item approximately $99.7\%$ of values lie within three standard deviations of the mean.
\end{itemize}
