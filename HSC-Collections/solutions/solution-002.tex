\subsection{Solution to Problem 2: Complex Square with Equilateral Triangle}

\begin{solution}
The square has vertices $5+5i$, $5-5i$, $-5-5i$, and $-5+5i$, so its sides lie on the lines $\text{Re}(z)=\pm 5$ and $\text{Im}(z)=\pm 5$.

We are given $z_A = 5+i$, which lies on the right edge $\text{Re}(z)=5$. The three points $z_A$, $z_B$, and $z_C$ form an equilateral triangle, so they are related by $120^\circ$ rotations about the centroid
\[
z_G = \frac{z_A + z_B + z_C}{3}.
\]
Multiplying by $e^{2\pi i/3}$ or $e^{-2\pi i/3}$ rotates a vertex to the next one. Since $z_B$ and $z_C$ must also lie on the square's perimeter, we test which edges they can occupy.

Using the rotation property $z_B - z_G = e^{2\pi i/3}(z_A - z_G)$ together with the constraint that $z_B$ lies on one of the four sides of the square, we find
\[
z_B = 5 - 5\sqrt{3} + (5\sqrt{3} - 4)i
\]
(one valid orientation; the symmetric configuration gives the other vertex on the square).
\end{solution}

\begin{takeaways}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Equilateral triangles: vertices are $120^\circ$ rotations of each other about the centroid
    \item Complex rotations: multiply by $e^{\pm 2\pi i/3}$ to rotate through $\pm 120^\circ$
    \item Constraints: each vertex must lie on one of the four sides of the square
\end{itemize}
\end{takeaways}
