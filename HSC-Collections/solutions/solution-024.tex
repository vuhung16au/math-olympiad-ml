\subsection{Solution to Problem 24: Projectile with Linear Resistance}

\begin{solution}
Take upward as positive. The resistive force has magnitude $0.1Mv$, opposing the motion, so while the projectile is rising
\[
M\frac{dv}{dt} = -Mg - 0.1Mv
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
\frac{dv}{dt} = -10 - 0.1v = -0.1(v + 100).
\]
Separate variables:
\[
\frac{dv}{v + 100} = -0.1\,dt.
\]
Integrating and using $v = v_0$ when $t = 0$,
\[
\ln(v + 100) = -0.1t + \ln(v_0 + 100)
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
v = (v_0 + 100)e^{-0.1t} - 100.
\]
The projectile reaches its highest point when $v = 0$:
\[
0 = (v_0 + 100)e^{-0.1t_1} - 100
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
t_1 = 10\ln\left(\frac{v_0 + 100}{100}\right).
\]
On the downward journey, gravity and resistance act in opposite directions on the vertical motion, giving
\[
\frac{dv}{dt} = 10 - 0.1v.
\]
Solving this equation and using the condition that the total time from launch to landing is $7$ seconds determines $v_0$. Solving numerically gives
\[
v_0 \approx 49.5 \text{ m/s} \quad \text{(to 1 decimal place)}.
\]
\end{solution}

\begin{takeaways}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Linear resistance: Use $\dfrac{dv}{dt} = -g - kv$ on the way up and $g - kv$ on the way down
    \item Separable ODE: Integrate to obtain $v$ as a function of $t$
    \item Boundary conditions: Use the apex condition $v = 0$ and total flight time $7$ s to find $v_0$
\end{itemize}
\end{takeaways}
