\subsection{Solution to Problem 4: Complex Triangle Inequality}

\begin{solution}
Let $r = |z| > 0$. We are given $\left|z - \dfrac{4}{z}\right| = 2$. Apply the triangle inequality in both directions.

\textbf{Lower bound.} For any complex numbers,
\[
\left|z - \frac{4}{z}\right| \ge \left||z| - \left|\frac{4}{z}\right|\right| = |r - \tfrac{4}{r}|.
\]
So $2 \ge r - \dfrac{4}{r}$ (since $r > 0$ and typically $r \ge \dfrac{4}{r}$ for large $|z|$). Multiplying by $r > 0$,
\[
2r \ge r^2 - 4
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
r^2 - 2r - 4 \le 0.
\]
The positive root of $r^2 - 2r - 4 = 0$ is $r = 1 + \sqrt{5}$, so
\[
|z| \le 1 + \sqrt{5}.
\]

\textbf{Upper bound.} Also,
\[
\left|z - \frac{4}{z}\right| \le |z| + \left|\frac{4}{z}\right| = r + \frac{4}{r}.
\]
So $2 \le r + \dfrac{4}{r}$, giving $r^2 - 2r + 4 \ge 0$. This quadratic has discriminant $(-2)^2 - 4(4) = -12 < 0$, so it is always true and gives no further restriction on $|z|$.

Combining the useful bound,
\[
|z| \le 1 + \sqrt{5} = \sqrt{5} + 1.
\]
\end{solution}

\begin{takeaways}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Triangle inequality: $|a-b| \ge \bigl||a|-|b|\bigr|$ and $|a-b| \le |a|+|b|$
    \item Substitute $|z|=r$ to obtain quadratic inequalities in $r$
    \item Only the lower-bound direction produces a finite upper limit on $|z|$
\end{itemize}
\end{takeaways}
