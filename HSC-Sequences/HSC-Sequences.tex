\section{Introduction}

\subsection{Project Overview}
This booklet is a focused companion for NSW HSC Mathematics Extension 1 students studying sequences and series. It is designed to connect core sequence skills to exam-style problem solving with clear methods and structured solutions.

\subsection{Target Audience}
This resource is written for Year 12 students in Extension 1, and for tutors and teachers who want a compact booklet with consistent notation and worked examples.

\subsection{How to Use This Booklet}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item Read the fundamentals review first, then attempt the detailed problems in Part 1.
  \item Use Part 2 for timed practice and fluency, using hints only when needed.
  \item Keep a formula sheet while you work, but always justify each step from first principles.
  \item Track common slips, especially sign errors in geometric sums and indexing mistakes in sigma notation.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Topics Covered}
This booklet develops the following Extension 1 ideas:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item defining sequences by term formula, recursion, and context
  \item arithmetic sequences and arithmetic series
  \item geometric sequences and geometric series
  \item finite sum formulas and sigma notation
  \item limiting sum of a geometric series when $|r|<1$
  \item links between recurring decimals and geometric series
  \item mixed AP/GP problem solving in HSC-style questions
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Where This Leads Next}
Sequence fluency supports later work in calculus, proof, and Extension 2 style reasoning. In particular, convergence arguments and recursive definitions form a bridge to harder topics such as iterative methods and infinite processes.

\section{Conclusion}
Strong sequence work in Extension 1 is not just memorizing formulas. It is about choosing the right model, controlling notation, and checking whether each step is logically justified.

The key habits are:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item define terms clearly, including first term and common difference or ratio
  \item check indexing before and after every sigma manipulation
  \item identify whether a question is about terms, partial sums, or limits
  \item test answers for reasonableness by checking growth and sign behavior
\end{itemize}

With steady practice, sequences become one of the most reliable scoring areas in Extension 1.
