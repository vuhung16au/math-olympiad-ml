\textbf{Concept / Model} & \textbf{Key Form / Formula} & \textbf{Key Interpretation} \\
\hline
General first-order DE & $\dfrac{dy}{dx} = F(x,y)$ & One unknown function; need one condition to fix the solution. \\
\hline
Initial condition & $y(x_0)=y_0$ & Picks out one particular solution from the family. \\
\hline
Separable DE & $A(y)\,dy = B(x)\,dx$ & Separate variables, then integrate both sides. \\
\hline
Equilibrium solution & $\dfrac{dy}{dx}=0 \;\Rightarrow\; y=k$ & Constant solution; solution curve is a horizontal line. \\
\hline
Slope field &
\begin{tikzpicture}[scale=0.28, baseline=(current bounding box.center)]
  \foreach \xi in {-2,-1,0,1,2}{
    \foreach \yi in {-2,-1,0,1,2}{
      \pgfmathsetmacro{\sl}{\xi+\yi}
      \pgfmathsetmacro{\ang}{atan(\sl)}
      \pgfmathsetmacro{\dx}{0.32*cos(\ang)}
      \pgfmathsetmacro{\dy}{0.32*sin(\ang)}
      \draw[gray!70,thin] (\xi-\dx,\yi-\dy)--(\xi+\dx,\yi+\dy);
    }
  }
  \draw[->] (-2.6,0)--(2.6,0) node[right,font=\tiny]{$x$};
  \draw[->] (0,-2.6)--(0,2.6) node[above,font=\tiny]{$y$};
  \node[font=\tiny,below] at (0,-2.6) {$\tfrac{dy}{dx}=x+y$};
\end{tikzpicture}
& Each segment shows the tangent direction of a solution curve at that point. \\
\hline
Exponential growth/decay & $\dfrac{dy}{dx}=ky \;\Rightarrow\; y=Ae^{kx}$ & $k>0$: growth; $k<0$: decay. $y=0$ is the only equilibrium. \\
\hline
Logistic growth & $\dfrac{dy}{dt}=ry\!\left(1-\dfrac{y}{M}\right)$ & Equilibria $y=0$ (unstable) and $y=M$ (stable); $M$ = carrying capacity. \\
\hline
Logistic with harvesting & $\dfrac{dy}{dt}=ry\!\left(1-\dfrac{y}{M}\right)-h$ & High $h$ removes the positive equilibrium; population can collapse. \\
\hline
Newton's law of cooling & $\dfrac{dT}{dt}=-k(T-T_a) \;\Rightarrow\; T=T_a+Ce^{-kt}$ & $T\to T_a$ as $t\to\infty$; $T_a$ is the only stable equilibrium. \\
\hline
Torricelli draining & $\dfrac{dh}{dt}=-k\sqrt{h} \;\Rightarrow\; 2\sqrt{h}=-kt+C$ & Draining rate slows as water level $h$ falls; empties in finite time. \\
\hline
Particle on a curve & $\mathbf{r}=x\,\mathbf{i}+y\,\mathbf{j}$,\quad $\mathbf{v}=\dot x\,\mathbf{i}+\dot y\,\mathbf{j}$,\quad $\mathbf{a}=\ddot x\,\mathbf{i}+\ddot y\,\mathbf{j}$ & Differentiate position to get velocity; differentiate again for acceleration. \\
