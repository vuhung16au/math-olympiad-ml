% Topic: Binomial Distributions

If $X \sim B(n, p)$, we say $X$ follows a \textbf{binomial distribution} with parameters $n$ (number of trials) and $p$ (probability of success).

\textbf{Mean and variance of the binomial distribution:}
\[
E(X) = np, \qquad \mathrm{Var}(X) = np(1-p).
\]

\textbf{Shape of the distribution:}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item When $p = 0.5$, the distribution is symmetric.
  \item When $p < 0.5$, the distribution is right-skewed (positively skewed).
  \item When $p > 0.5$, the distribution is left-skewed (negatively skewed).
  \item As $n$ increases, the distribution becomes more bell-shaped.
\end{itemize}

\begin{remark}
The binomial distribution is the most important discrete distribution in the HSC syllabus. Make sure you can correctly identify $n$, $p$, and $r$ before substituting into the formula.
\end{remark}
