% Topic: Mean and Variance of a Distribution

\textbf{Mean (Expected Value).} The mean of a continuous random variable $X$ with pdf $f(x)$ is
\[
\mu = E(X) = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} x\,f(x)\,dx.
\]

\textbf{Variance.} The variance measures the spread of the distribution:
\[
\sigma^2 = \mathrm{Var}(X) = E\!\left[(X - \mu)^2\right] = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} (x-\mu)^2\,f(x)\,dx.
\]
An equivalent and often more convenient formula is
\[
\mathrm{Var}(X) = E(X^2) - [E(X)]^2.
\]

\textbf{Standard deviation.} $\sigma = \sqrt{\mathrm{Var}(X)}.$

\textbf{Properties of expectation:}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item $E(aX + b) = aE(X) + b$ for constants $a$, $b$.
  \item $\mathrm{Var}(aX + b) = a^2\,\mathrm{Var}(X)$.
\end{itemize}
