\section{The standard normal distribution}
\subsection*{A brief summary of the standard normal probability distribution}

\noindent
The figure below shows the \textit{standard normal probability density function} $y = \phi(z)$.
The shaded region to the left of a vertical boundary at~$z$ corresponds to the \textit{cumulative distribution function}
\[
    \Phi(z) = P(Z \le z) = \int_{-\infty}^z \phi(t) \, dt.
\]
The table further down gives some values of $\Phi(z) = P(Z \le z)$. For example,
\[
    P(Z \le 1.6) = \Phi(1.6) = \int_{-\infty}^{1.6} \phi(z) \, dz \approx 0.9452.
\]

\end{tikzpicture}
\end{center}

\noindent
For many calculations the \textit{empirical rule} (68--95--99.7) is sufficient:
\begin{align*}
    P(-1 \le Z \le 1) &\approx 68\% \\
    P(-2 \le Z \le 2) &\approx 95\% \\
    P(-3 \le Z \le 3) &\approx 99.7\%
\end{align*}
